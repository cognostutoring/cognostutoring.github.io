Why Cognos Tutoring

We don't assign random tutors or follow one-size-fits-all plans. Every student is intentionally matched with the right tutor and program based on how they learn, what they need, and what they're working toward.

Personalized Matching

Clear Progress & Communication

Meet The Team

Aryan Birajdar

Aryan Birajdar is a co-founder of Cognos Tutoring and an incoming Electrical Engineering student at Purdue University. With a 1550 SAT score, first-place recognition at DECA States, and years of leadership experience as an Eagle Scout and student leader, Aryan understands the dedication and strategies required to succeed both inside and outside the classroom.

Beyond academics, Aryan has conducted research alongside a University of Connecticut professor and has experience working directly with students as an instructor at Mathnasium. Through both mentorship and teaching, he has developed a passion for making challenging concepts approachable and helping students build lasting confidence. At Cognos Tutoring, Aryan is committed to helping students strengthen their skills, develop effective study habits, and achieve meaningful academic growth.

Diego Aguilar

Diego Aguilar is a co-founder of Cognos Tutoring and an incoming Mechanical Engineering student at Northeastern University, Boston. With two years of professionally mentored engineering research, Diego's work has earned top honors at the Connecticut Science and Engineering Fair, along with recognition from the Connecticut Home Builders Association for his work in Mechanical, Civil, and Architectural engineering. Furthermore, he is a silver medalist at the Yale Science Olympiad competition, showing skills in collaborative and academic environments. His background and experiences allow him to help students master complex coursework, along with competitive research and presentation projects.

Arnav Kamineni

Arnav is a co-founder of Cognos Tutoring and an incoming freshman at the University of California, Berkeley, with over five years of experience teaching students across all K–12 grade levels. A U.S. Presidential Scholars Program nominee and 1st place finisher at Lockheed Martin's CodeQuest, he understands what it takes to compete and excel at the highest level. As President of his school's Math Team and a computational biology researcher at UConn, Arnav brings that same dedication to every student he works with, helping them grow into sharper thinkers and stronger performers.
